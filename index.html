PUBLICATIONS

The Theory of Relativity (1905)

This groundbreaking paper introduced the theory of relativity, revolutionizing our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

General Theory of Relativity (1915)

In this seminal work, Einstein formulated his theory of general relativity, providing a new framework for understanding gravitational phenomena.

Special Theory of Relativity (1905)

This foundational paper laid the groundwork for the special theory of relativity, presenting concepts such as time dilation and length contraction.

Photoelectric Effect (1905)

Einstein's research on the photoelectric effect demonstrated the particle-like nature of light, contributing to the development of quantum theory.

Bose-Einstein Statistics (1924)

In collaboration with Satyendra Nath Bose, Einstein developed the theory of Bose-Einstein condensation, which describes the behavior of particles at extremely low temperatures.

EPR Paradox (1935)

The Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox, proposed by Einstein, Podolsky, and Rosen in 1935, raised Questions about the completeness of quantum mechanics and spurred debates about the nature of reality.
